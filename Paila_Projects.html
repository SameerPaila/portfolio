Scratch Project

Project: Scratch Project
Course: CSE (Computer Science and Engineering: Entry Level Course)
Duration: About 3 weeks (August-September)
Objective: Make an interactive game or story using the Scratch programming language
Type of Project: Partner project (2 people)

For the scratch project, my partner and I began by going over the basic tutorial for the programming language. This tutorial introduced us to the multiple variable roles in Scratch and it also familiarized us with the block oriented code that Scratch uses. After the tutorial was completed, we were told by our instructor that our project would involve creating an original story or game for people to play. We began by delegating tasks to each member and creating a project notebook which would be used on a daily basis to keep track of our progress. We brainstormed different ideas and concluded that it would be best to program an RPG (role-playing game) with multiple levels and enemies with different characteristics because that was what appealed to us the most. Then, we presented our ideas in a professional manner to another group in the class to get valuable feedback and improve our final product. The last part of the pre-programming process was completed after a flowchart was designed to clearly display our ideas and prioritize them under different tiers to provide us with an outline of what we wanted to do. The daily log, flowchart, brainstorming session, feedback session, and more can be viewed on the project notebook for further information regarding the design process. My partner and I scored well on this project because the game was fully functional, easy-to-play and practical, and most parts of the rubric were followed. Take a look at the game we created! It can be played to the right. To play, use the arrow keys to move the character, press the s and d keys to toggle player stats display, click on enemies to inflict damage, and move the character to the right edge of the screen to advance to the next level.

MIT App Inventor Project

Project: App Inventor Project
Course: CSE (Computer Science and Engineering: Entry Level Course)
Duration: About 3 weeks (September-October)
Objective: Make an app or game using MIT app inventor
Type of Project: Partner project (2 people)

For the MIT app inventor project, my partner and I were assigned with the task of improving and extending upon a game given to us from a range of basic tutorials. My partner and I began the project by brainstorming some ideas and finding which tutorial best suited our ideas. Since we wanted to create a game with multiple levels, enemies, and objectives, we believed that the Get The Gold for AI2 tutorial would be the best layout to start with. Similar to the scratch project, we created a project notebook and after brainstorming ideas for what our game should include, we presented them to another group who listened to our pitch and gave us some helpful suggestions. The flowchart for this project was used to organize our thoughts and feedback to develop a final app which incorporated all of the desired aspects. A daily log was also used to keep track of individual and group progress which was useful in determining what aspects of the game we wanted to prioritize and what aspects could be saved for future development. My partner and I scored decently well on this project because we met the majority of the requirements mentioned in the rubric and the app appeared to work the majority of the time when it was downloaded and played on a mobile device. Python Text Adventure Project

Project: Python Text Adventure Project
Course: CSE (Computer Science and Engineering: Entry Level course)
Duration: About 1.5 weeks (January 11th - January 22nd)
Objective: Create an original text-based adventure story/game based on a theme, topic, or idea
Type of Project: Partner Project (2 people)

For the Python Text Adventure Project ,my partner and I were assigned with the task of using the python programming language to develop a text-based interactive game or story. Since this assignment was only considered a 'mini-project', no notebook was maintained. Instead, all of our ideas were directly collected on a flowchart. To begin the project, we brainstormed by selecting different themes or topics that could be implemented in humorous and creative way. After brainstorming, we decided to base our project around a story involving scammers. We chose this theme because not only was it original and creative, but it also allowed us to add many options and pathways for the player to choose from. After developing our flowchart and coming to a consensus on the different portions of the story, we immediately began programming. To avoid too many nested if-else structures, most of our code was dominated by individual if-else structures and functions which were called upon. Since we gave ourselves more time to program, we were able to successfully complete all aspects of our story that were mentioned on the flowchart. My partner and I received a perfect score on this project because all the requirements listed in the rubric were met. A picture of our text-adventure code can be seen to the right.

Mechanical Winch Project

Project: Mechanical Winch Project
Course: POE (Principles of Engineering: Foundational Course)
Duration: 1 week (October 17th, 2018 - October 24th, 2018)
Objective: Design a fully functional mechanical winch that can lift at least 100 grams to a height of at least 30 cm over the side of a table using a FisherTech motor, VEX parts, and electrical circuitry.

For the Mechanical Winch Project , we were put into groups of four to make a prototype of an electrically powered winch system that could lift at least 100 grams to a height of at least 30 cm over the side of a table. More details listing the constraints of the project, the client being marketed to, and the instructions for the NetLogo program are given here. Since this project was very short, we immediately began by brainstorming ideas and figuring out a way to mount the FisherTech motor while simultaneously making it compatible with the VEX gears and metal parts. After brainstorming, as the project manager for our group, I completed the group responsibilities form and delegated tasks regarding the building of the prototype and the coding of the NetLogo simulation to my team members. As an individual contributor, I assisted my team in developing the series circuit that would be used to connect our battery-powered source to the motor which would provide power to rotate the winch. Furthermore, by utilizing the NetLogo code help page and prior programming knowledge, I developed the simulation code that modeled the relationship between power, winch drum size, weight of the object being lifted, and speed at which the object would be lifted. Once the program was completed and the prototype was tested multiple times, measurements of the mechanism's performance were noted in the project documentation in order to keep a record of our team's progress and complete deliverables that would eventually be presented to the client. Overall, my team scored extremely well on this project because we met most of the project requirements as per the rubric. A picture of our prototype can be seen to the right.
